About Us

LOKETA AGRO-FARM is Sina loketa startup project in bidi bidi registered in Uganda as limited by guarantee in 2020 with the aim of engaging local farmers in refugee camps and host communities to practice Agro farming, value addition to agricultural raw materials (food processing), that is, food crops and cash crops, that are eco-friendly. Loketa agrofarming was founded through the challenges I faced as a refugee in Bidi bidi with my fellow colleagues in the settlement due to food shortage and high unemployment rate.

The world is facing a lot of challenges with food insecurity as a result of climate change, low farm productivity and global growing population despite the advancement in technology. “Due to above problem, Refugees in Bidi Bidi settlement in Yumbe district (Uganda) who were monthly been supported or depending on UNHCR were decried lack of beans and peas to supplement their food and yet beans are the main source of proteins. Currently, the World Food Program-WFP through Office of the Prime Minister OPM gives Maize Grains and Sorghum without beans and oil due to shortage in supply of beans and cooking oil. According to some of the refugees, they have now resorted to selling the grains to buy beans while others have sold some of their household items to supplement their need for saurce to accompany their food. “Refugees in bidi bidi- decry lack of staple foods (independent.co.ug) In addition to the above, the only favorable crops that can yield fast in the settlement are simsim,sunflowers and gnuts were majority of the refugee are adapted to grow oil crops due to the climate change which has led to low market price to the produce. Addressing these problems requires innovative solutionthat ensures adoption of more effective production systems; retraining farmers on modern farming and innovation; encouraging stronger youth engagement in the sub-sector; value addition facilities and restructuring markets for optimum profitability. Minimizing low agricultural produce as an effective way to increase food availability, ensure food security, promote consumption of healthy diets which can create wealth for refugee farmers and relevant actors involved across the crop value chains. Loketa Agro- farm seeks to fill this gap by engaging local farmers in the refugee and host communities to practice agro farming on small land, value addition to agricultural raw materials (food processing), and i.e. food crops and cash crops, that are eco-friendly which can enable them easily to sustain themselves than depending on UNHCR on monthly bases. FARMING ACTIVITIES TO BE PRACTICE

  1. food crops like cassava, maize, sorghum rice, millet etc.

GOALS AND OBJECTIVES

The main objectives of the project are to;

    ➢ Contribute to the alleviation of poverty in refugee communities.
    ➢ Contribute to the improvement of health and social-economic conditions in the settlement
    ➢ To promote sustainable development initiatives, creativity, self-reliance and responsibility in the communities
    ➢ To establish the space and train a core contingent of 50 women in different ways to practice how to make agrofarming as the core method to sustain their family.
    ➢ To introduce 70 local farmers (members of widows’ association) from Bidi bidi settlement to learn how to add value to agricultural produce and making it as a means of providing personal and collective income and reducing poverty within the community.
    ➢ To provide financial resources for the work of the youth and women through Leadership training, project management, proposal writing etc.
    ➢ To establish an innovative model of microeconomic enterprise which can be replicated.
    ➢ Employment creation in biddi-biddi through value addition to agricultural raw materials.
    ➢ High disposable income in refugee settlement through processing of variety of food items.
    ➢ Increased demand for goods and service in the refugee camps. In other parts of the settlement.
    ➢ To reduce environmental degradation through the use of internal organic agriculture produce.

OUTCOMES

    • Refugees will become self-reliable through the food processing on agricultural produce making retail business.
    • Improved health standards of the refugees through the use of affordable products produced.
